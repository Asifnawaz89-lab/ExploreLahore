Historical Background

Though its origins date back to antiquity, the present structure of the Lahore Fort was largely built during the reign of Mughal Emperor Akbar in the 16th century, with major additions by Emperors Jahangir, Shah Jahan, and Aurangzeb. Over time, it served as a royal residence and military stronghold.

In 1981, the fort was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, along with the nearby Shalimar Gardens.
