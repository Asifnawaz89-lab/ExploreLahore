Minar-e-Pakistan – The National Monument of Pakistan

Minar-e-Pakistan

Overview

Located in Iqbal Park, Lahore, Minar-e-Pakistan stands as a powerful symbol of Pakistan's independence movement. This historic monument marks the spot where the Pakistan Resolution was passed on 23rd March 1940, laying the foundation for the creation of Pakistan.

Historical Significance

Minar-e-Pakistan commemorates the Lahore Resolution, passed by the All-India Muslim League under the leadership of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah. The resolution demanded a separate homeland for Muslims of the Indian subcontinent — an idea that became reality seven years later in 1947.

Architectural Details

Designed by Nasreddin Murat-Khan, a Pakistani-Russian architect, and constructed between 1960 and 1968, the structure blends Mughal, Islamic, and modern architectural styles.

  • Height: Approximately 70 meters (230 feet) tall
  • Base Structure: Shaped like a blooming flower, symbolizing growth and freedom
  • Four Platforms: Each representing stages of the freedom struggle, from slavery to independence
  • Calligraphy: The base features inscriptions of the Lahore Resolution in Urdu, Bengali, English, and Arabic, along with quotes from Quaid-e-Azam and Allama Iqbal

Cultural Importance

Minar-e-Pakistan is more than just a monument—it’s a national icon. It is the centerpiece of many national celebrations, public gatherings, and patriotic events. The site holds emotional and historical value for every Pakistani, reminding us of the sacrifices made for freedom.

Tips for Visitors

  • Best time to visit: Evening or early morning
  • Parking is available nearby
  • Don’t forget to take water and wear comfortable shoes

Visiting Minar-e-Pakistan

Located in one of Lahore’s largest parks, Minar-e-Pakistan is surrounded by fountains, gardens, and walkways—making it a peaceful spot for families, students, and tourists.

  • 📍 Location: Iqbal Park, Lahore
  • 🕰 Visiting Hours: Open daily till evening
  • 🎟 Entry: Free for all visitors

Why You Should Visit

Whether you are a tourist discovering Pakistan’s history or a citizen seeking inspiration, Minar-e-Pakistan offers a powerful connection to the country’s roots. It’s not just a monument — it’s the birthplace of a nation’s dream.
